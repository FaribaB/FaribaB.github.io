Why was Pwidlé formed?

Zelda

The dog that made all of this possible.

On Sunday 2nd August, 2020, my dog, Zelda, passed away because of anaemia and tick fever. This horrible mishap could have been prevented if her veterinarian had made a proper diagnosis when I took Zelda to her clinic for a review after I noticed a change in her behaviour and daily routine. Due to her lack of qualifications and experience, even negligence, the veterinarian disregarded the signs which were evidently visible. If the required corrective actions were taken, my pet, Zelda, could have been alive today. This dilemma has greatly impacted the way in which I view the level of service offered by various organizations. As such, I firmly believed that quality, care and due diligence should always be a part of a service provider's policy. In this regard, this organization was named after Zelda's nickname, Pwidlé. It was formed in order to spread awareness about how unqualified members in a workplace can negatively you, your community or your country on a whole.










How will Pwidlé benefit you?

Pwidlé will be of direct benefit to you as an individual and by extension, it will benefit your community overall. Furthermore, your country will also attain socio-economic growth. It is an organization that would be able to monitor workplaces for poor quality of service delivery and receive complaints with the aims of conformity. In order to spread awareness, Pwidlé will also host community gatherings to teach and inform the citizen about the qualifications for certain jobs and the disadvantages of employing an unqualified person. Pwidlé will create a specified unit to train and recruit job seekers for specified positions to suit their age, skill set, exxperience, educational attainment and even their passion. Thus, when organizations require applicants to fill vacant positions, Pwidlé can be engaged to ensure that the right people get the right jobs to maintain effectiveness, quality standards and efficiency.

Pwidlé will be extremely advantageous to:
  • you as a person by giving you a sense of relief and sureness to you as a job seeker, a service provider or a service receiver.
  • the members of your community by allowing the unqualified businesses there to better themselves so that, now, the services in your community are qualified and will leave you happy. It will also provide training and will aid in the employment of the members of your community who are qualified for work but however do not have a job due to saturation in that job.
  • the country on a whole by seeking to maximise and increase the possible level of high quality skill- based employees which intern will boost the economic growth of the country.
